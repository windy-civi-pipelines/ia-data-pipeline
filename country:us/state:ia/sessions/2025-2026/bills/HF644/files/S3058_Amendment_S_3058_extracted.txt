6 NONENACTMENT OF LEGISLATION
7 Section 1. Section 232.2, subsection 12, paragraph c, Code
8 2025, is amended to read as follows:
9 c. (1) All rights and duties of a custodian shall be
10 subject to any residual rights and duties remaining in a parent
11 or guardian.
12 (2) Notwithstanding subparagraph (1), the department may
13 consent to emergency medical care and routine medical care for
14 a child that will allow the child to experience childhood and
15 participate in activities in a manner similar to the child’s
16 peers. “Routine medical care” includes but is not limited to
17 all of the following:
18 (a) Preventative care, also known as wellness care, not
19 including the administration of a vaccination.
20 (b) Nonemergency medical care including but not limited
21 to a physical examination, a diagnostic laboratory test, or a
22 medical visit for a minor illness.
23 (c) Use of necessary medication, including but not limited
24 to antibiotics.
25 (3) Notwithstanding subparagraph (1), the department
26 may consent to a child’s participation in an individualized
27 education program.
28 Sec. 2. Section 232.2, subsection 12, Code 2025, is amended
29 by adding the following new paragraph:
30 NEW PARAGRAPH. d. If a court order entered under this
31 chapter transfers legal custody of a child to the department,
32 the department shall have the authority to delegate the
33 department’s rights and duties as custodian of the child to a
34 licensee under chapter 237.
35 Sec. 3. Section 237.10, subsection 1, Code 2025, is amended
