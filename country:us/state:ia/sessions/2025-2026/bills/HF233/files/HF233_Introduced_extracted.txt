Under current law, a driver making 8 a left turn must yield the right-of-way to all vehicles 9 approaching from the opposite direction which are within the 10 intersection or so close thereto as to constitute an immediate 11 hazard, before making the left turn (Code section 321.320). 12 In addition, the driver of a vehicle making a left turn is 13 required to depart from the intersection to the right of 14 the center line of the roadway being entered (Code section 15 321.311). The bill requires the driver to also keep the 16 vehicle as close as practicable to the center line. 17 If the left turn is from a two-way street into a one-way 18 street, the bill requires the driver, after entering the 19 intersection, to depart from the intersection as close as 20 practicable to the left-hand curb or edge of the street being 21 entered. If the left turn is from a one-way street into a 22 two-way street, the bill requires the driver to make the turn 23 as close as practicable to the left-hand curb or edge of the 24 street and, after entering the intersection, to depart from the 25 intersection as close as practicable to the center line of the 26 street being entered. 27 The bill requires a driver making a turn at an intersection 28 from a laned highway into another laned highway to maintain and 29 complete the turn by approaching, entering, and departing the 30 intersection in the same corresponding lane.
