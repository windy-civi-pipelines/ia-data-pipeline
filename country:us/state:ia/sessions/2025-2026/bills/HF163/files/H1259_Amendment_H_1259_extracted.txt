6 1. For purposes of this section:

8 (a) A criminal or juvenile justice agency as defined in

10 (b) A city, county, or township.
11 (c) A state agency.
12 (d) Any service or support provider that contracts with an
13 entity described in subparagraph divisions (a) through (c).
14 (2) “Covered entity” does not include the judicial branch.
15 b. “School” means all of the following:
16 (1) A school corporation organized under chapter 274.
17 (2) A nonpublic school as defined in section 280.2.
18 (3) A charter school established pursuant to chapter 256E.
19 (4) A charter school or innovation zone school established
20 pursuant to chapter 256F.
21 (5) Any service or support provider that contracts with an
22 entity described in subparagraphs (1) through (4).
23 c. “State agency” means a department, board, bureau,
24 commission, or other agency or authority of the state of Iowa.

26 including section 22.7, any covered entity or school that
27 provides services to students enrolled in kindergarten through
28 grade twelve who are experiencing or at risk of an emotional
29 disturbance or mental illness, or who pose an articulable and
30 significant threat to the health and safety of any person,
31 shall, upon the request of another covered entity or school
32 when the covered entity or school is acting as part of a
33 multidisciplinary school safety assessment team established
34 pursuant to section 280.36, share records or information that
35 are reasonably necessary to ensure access to appropriate

7 Sec. 4. NEW SECTION. 280.36 Multidisciplinary school safety
8 assessment teams —— immunity.
9 1. The board of directors of each school district and the

11 establish a multidisciplinary school safety assessment team.
12 If established, the multidisciplinary school safety assessment
13 team shall coordinate resources and assess and intervene when a
14 student enrolled in the school district or accredited nonpublic

17 employees of the school district or accredited nonpublic
18 school, or other student enrolled in the school district or
19 accredited nonpublic school.

21 consist of any of the following members:
22 a. A local law enforcement official.

24 c. A mental health professional.
25 d. A social services representative.
26 e. A school official.

32 team that shall coordinate resources among the schools and

34 exhibits behavior that may pose a threat to the safety of
35 either school, employees of either school, or students enrolled
