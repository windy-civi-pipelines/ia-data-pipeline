24 with chief information security officer.
25 In the interest of facilitating communication between
26 the chief information security officer and other entities
27 concerning security incidents and security breaches, all such
28 communications and any documents generated based in whole or in
29 part on such communications are confidential. Notwithstanding
30 chapter 22 or any other provision of law to the contrary, the
31 department shall not release such communications pursuant to
32 state open records laws, and such communications shall not be
33 received into evidence, subject to discovery, or otherwise
34 used in a trial, hearing, or other proceeding in or before any
35 court, regulatory body, or other authority of the state or a
HF 1028.2029.S (1) 91
-7- mb 7/14
1 political subdivision of the state, unless the communications
2 are subject to a protective order that prohibits further
3 disclosure of such communications and requires any court
4 filings of such communications to be made under seal. It is
5 the intent of the general assembly that these prohibitions and
6 restrictions also apply to federal courts, regulatory bodies,
7 and other authorities and for purposes of federal open records
8 laws, to the extent allowed by federal law and court rules.
9 The chief information security officer shall not release such
10 communications other than for any of the following purposes:
11 1. Identifying a cybersecurity threat, including the source
12 of the cybersecurity threat, or a security vulnerability, and
13 then only to government officials for purposes of addressing
14 the threat.
15 2. Responding to, or otherwise preventing or mitigating,
16 a specific threat of death, serious bodily harm, or serious
17 economic harm.
18 3. Responding to, investigating, prosecuting, or otherwise
19 preventing or mitigating a serious threat to a minor, including
20 sexual exploitation and threats to physical safety.
21 4. Preventing, investigating, disrupting, or prosecuting an
22 offense under state or federal law.
23 5. Providing a confidential cybersecurity briefing to the
24 governor or a member of the general assembly.
