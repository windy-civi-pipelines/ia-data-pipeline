4 <Section 1. Section 321.376, subsections 1 and 3, Code 2025,
5 are amended by striking the subsections and inserting in lieu
6 thereof the following:
7 1. a. In addition to meeting the qualifications under
8 section 321.375, a driver of a school bus shall hold a
9 commercial driver’s license issued by the department of
10 transportation with a passenger endorsement and a school bus
11 endorsement.
12 b. If required by the school district, a school bus driver
13 shall successfully complete an approved course of instruction
14 for school bus drivers in accordance with subsection 3A.
15 c. A person holding a temporary restricted license issued
16 under chapter 321J shall not operate a school bus.
17 3. A person applying for employment or employed as a school
18 bus driver shall not be required to successfully complete an
19 approved course of instruction for school bus drivers unless
20 the school district for which the person drives a school bus
21 requires successful completion of such course.
22 Sec. 2. Section 321.376, Code 2025, is amended by adding the
23 following new subsection:
24 NEW SUBSECTION. 3A. a. A school district may require
25 successful completion of an approved course of instruction for
26 school bus drivers. An approved course of instruction for
27 school bus drivers includes one or more of the following:
28 (1) A course of instruction for school bus drivers approved
29 by the department of education.
30 (2) Entry-level driver training completed in accordance
31 with section 321.188, subsection 1, paragraph “c”, if such
32 training meets the requirements of 49 C.F.R. pt. 380, Appendix
33 C, and 49 C.F.R. pt. 380, Appendix D.
34 (a) The department of education, in collaboration with
35 the department of transportation, shall adopt rules pursuant
HF 395.1789.S (2) 91
-1- mb 1/3
1 to chapter 17A on or before July 1, 2026, establishing
2 requirements for providers of entry-level driver training to
3 comply with this subparagraph.
4 (b) The rules must provide for minimum training content,
5 contact hours, and proficiency standards, and must establish a
6 standard training course duration that ensures each training
7 course provides all entry-level driver training requirements
8 and specified school bus driver training requirements,
9 including but not limited to training necessary for a passenger
10 endorsement and a school bus endorsement.
11 (3) Other training as described in a resolution adopted by
12 the school district, which may include but is not limited to
13 certain portions of a course described in subparagraph (1) or
14 (2).
15 b. If a school district requires successful completion of an
16 approved course of instruction for school bus drivers, a school
17 bus driver shall complete the course before or within the first
18 six months of employment and at least every twenty-four months
19 thereafter.
20 c. If a school bus driver is required to complete an
21 approved course of instruction for school bus drivers under
22 paragraph “a”, subparagraph (1), and fails to provide an
23 employer with a certificate of completion of the approved
24 course, the employer shall report the failure to the department
25 of education and the department shall revoke the driver’s
26 authorization to operate a school bus. The department of
27 education shall send notice of the revocation to both the
28 person whose school bus authorization has been revoked and
29 the employer. A person whose school bus authorization has
30 been revoked under this paragraph shall not be issued another
31 authorization until certification of the completion of an
32 approved course is received by the department of education,
33 or until the person becomes a school bus driver in a school
34 district that does not require successful completion of an
35 approved course of instruction for school bus drivers under
HF 395.1789.S (2) 91

1 paragraph “a”, subparagraph (1), and the person is otherwise in
