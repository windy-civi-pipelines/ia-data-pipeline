Under current law, the state racing and gaming 6 commission (commission) is required to conduct a socioeconomic 7 study on the impact of gambling on Iowans every eight years. 8 The bill identifies certain topics the commission must examine 9 for this socioeconomic study, and prohibits the commission 10 from issuing a new license on or after July 1, 2030, until the 11 required report on the socioeconomic study has been issued by 12 the commission for 2029. 13 The bill places a moratorium on the issuance of new licenses 14 to conduct gambling games in excess of 19 licenses from January 15 1, 2025, through June 30, 2030. The bill restricts gambling 16 games licenses to those counties conducting gambling games on 17 January 1, 2025. The bill allows the commission to authorize a 18 gambling games licensee to move to a new location within the 19 same county, to issue a gambling games license in a county if 20 a licensed facility in that county is sold, and to issue a new 21 license in a county if a previous license to conduct gambling 22 games in that county was surrendered, not renewed, or revoked. 23 Before a license is granted, the bill requires an operator 24 of an excursion gambling boat to work with the economic 25 development authority to encourage tourism from other states. 26 The bill prohibits the commission from issuing a new gambling 27 games license to an excursion gambling boat if the new 28 license would impact an existing licensee located in a county 29 contiguous to the Iowa border or a rural county (a county 30 with a population less than 30,000). 31 The bill prohibits the commission from issuing a new 32 gambling games license if the new license would negatively 33 impact the adjusted gross receipts of an existing gambling 34 games licensee by more than 10 percent, or negatively impact 35 -4- LSB 1488SV (1) 91 ll/ns 4/ 5
