12 EXPLANATION
13 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
14 the explanation’s substance by the members of the general assembly.
15 This bill relates to education, including by modifying
16 provisions related to community college and department of
17 education (DE) reporting requirements, the national board
18 certification pilot project, and employees of the Iowa
19 educational services for the blind and visually impaired
20 program or the Iowa school for the deaf.
21 DIVISION I —— REPORTING REQUIREMENTS. Current law requires
22 the state board of education to adopt rules directing the
23 community colleges to submit certain specified data from the
24 most recent fiscal year to the community colleges bureau using
25 criteria determined by the community colleges bureau. The
26 bill modifies these provisions to instead require the data to
27 be submitted directly to DE. The bill makes several other
28 modifications related to data submitted by the community
29 colleges to DE, and related to data submitted by DE to the
30 state board and the general assembly, including modifications
31 related to the information to be included in the annual
32 condition of community colleges report.
33 DIVISION II —— NATIONAL BOARD CERTIFICATION PROGRAM AND
34 STIPENDS. The bill changes the name of the national board
35 certification pilot project established in Code section 256.44
LSB 1430XD (5) 91
-4- jda/jh 4/5
S.F. _____ H.F. _____
1 to the national board certification program.
2 The bill provides that funds appropriated for purposes
3 of the student achievement and teacher quality program
4 that are allocated to DE for the issuance of national board
5 certification awards that remain unencumbered or unobligated
6 at the close of the fiscal year shall not revert but shall
7 be available for expenditure by DE for purposes of providing
8 stipends to individuals who receive an advanced dyslexia
9 specialist endorsement from the board of educational examiners
10 (BOEE). This provision takes effect upon enactment.
11 DIVISION III —— IOWA EDUCATIONAL SERVICES FOR THE BLIND AND
12 VISUALLY IMPAIRED PROGRAM AND THE IOWA SCHOOL FOR THE DEAF.
13 The bill provides that Code section 279.27 (discharge of a
14 teacher) applies to employees of the Iowa educational services
15 for the blind and visually impaired program and employees
16 of the Iowa school for the deaf, whether or not licensed by
17 the BOEE, who are responsible for the care, instruction, or
18 supervision of children.
19 The bill requires the department of administrative services,
20 in consultation with DE, to establish an annual compensation
21 schedule applicable to all employees of the Iowa educational
22 services for the blind and visually impaired program and all
23 employees of the Iowa school for the deaf and to set the
24 employee benefits for such employees.
