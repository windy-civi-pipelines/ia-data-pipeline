23 EXPLANATION
24 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
25 the explanation’s substance by the members of the general assembly.
26 This bill relates to the administration of elections.
27 The bill assigns responsibility for investigating election
28 misconduct reported to the attorney general to the county
29 sheriff or other law enforcement agency with jurisdiction.
30 Upon completion of the investigation, the bill requires the law
31 enforcement agency to report the results of the investigation
32 to the attorney general, in addition to the state commissioner
33 of elections as required under current law. Information in
34 the possession of or requested by the state commissioner of
35 elections as part of an investigation remains a confidential
LSB 1422XD (3) 91
-6- ss/ns 6/8
S.F. _____ H.F. _____
1 record.
2 The bill specifies that candidates for federal office are
3 not required to affirm on an affidavit of candidacy that the
4 candidate is aware that the candidate is disqualified from
5 holding office if the candidate has been convicted of a felony
6 or other infamous crime and the candidate’s rights have not
7 been restored by the governor or by the president of the United
8 States. The bill directs the state commissioner of elections
9 to create new forms to be used by candidates for federal
10 office.
11 The bill allows the state commissioner of elections to
12 issue directives that are not subject to the administrative
13 rulemaking process to clarify election laws and rules.
14 The bill broadens the scope of records held by a county
15 commissioner of elections that the state commissioner of
16 elections may examine to ensure compliance with requirements
17 relating to the conduct of elections. The bill requires
18 a county commissioner of elections to inform the state
19 commissioner of elections if the county commissioner’s office
20 is involved in physical or cybersecurity assessments performed
21 by a federal or state agency or other entity. The bill
22 allows commissioners of elections to adopt minimum security
23 protocols that must be complied with by vendors utilized by
24 the commissioners of elections and to limit access to election
25 infrastructure if a vendor does not comply with the security
26 protocols.
27 The bill strikes a provision allowing a county commissioner
28 of elections to choose whether to use automatic tabulating
29 equipment in certain elections and makes conforming changes.
30 The bill allows a county commissioner of elections to use an
31 electronic election register or poll book in lieu of a paper
32 register or poll book if the electronic election register or
33 poll book is a product that has been certified for use in the
34 state by the state commissioner of elections. The bill directs
35 the state commissioner of elections to adopt rules regarding
LSB 1422XD (3) 91

S.F. _____ H.F. _____
1 electronic election registers and poll books, which must
2 include operational and security standards.
3 The bill allows the state commissioner of elections to call
4 for a recount of any election and advise recount boards.
5 The bill limits objections that may be made to the validity
6 of presidential nominees to the legal sufficiency of the
7 certificate of nomination. Certificates of nomination shall
8 be presumed valid.

24 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with

26 This bill relates to the administration of elections.
27 The bill assigns responsibility for investigating election
28 misconduct reported to the attorney general to the county
29 sheriff or other law enforcement agency with jurisdiction.
30 Upon completion of the investigation, the bill requires the law
31 enforcement agency to report the results of the investigation
32 to the attorney general, in addition to the state commissioner
33 of elections as required under current law. Information in
34 the possession of or requested by the state commissioner of
35 elections as part of an investigation remains a confidential

2 The bill specifies that candidates for federal office are
3 not required to affirm on an affidavit of candidacy that the
4 candidate is aware that the candidate is disqualified from
5 holding office if the candidate has been convicted of a felony
6 or other infamous crime and the candidate’s rights have not
7 been restored by the governor or by the president of the United

11 The bill allows the state commissioner of elections to
