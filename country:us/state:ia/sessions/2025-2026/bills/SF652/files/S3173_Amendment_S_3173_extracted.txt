1 Amend Senate File 652 as follows:
3 inserting:
4 <Section 1. Section 15A.1, subsection 1, paragraph b, Code
5 2025, is amended to read as follows:
6 b. For purposes of this chapter, “economic development”
7 means private or joint public and private investment involving
8 the creation of new jobs and income or the retention of
9 existing jobs and income that would otherwise be lost or the
10 provision of workforce housing.
11 Sec. 2. Section 15A.1, subsection 2, Code 2025, is amended
12 by adding the following new paragraph:
13 NEW PARAGRAPH. e. Development policies that advance the
14 development of workforce housing.
15 Sec. 3. Section 403.17, subsection 14, Code 2025, is amended
16 to read as follows:
17 14. “Low or and moderate income families” means those
18 families, including single person households, earning no
19 more than eighty percent of the higher of the median family
20 income of the county or the statewide nonmetropolitan area as
21 determined by the latest United States department of housing
22 and urban development, section 8 income guidelines.
23 Sec. 4. Section 403.17, Code 2025, is amended by adding the
24 following new subsection:
25 NEW SUBSECTION. 14A. “Low and moderate income family
26 housing” means housing for low and moderate income families and
27 includes housing that meets the requirements of section 15.353.
28 Sec. 5. Section 403.22, subsection 1, Code 2025, is amended
29 by adding the following new paragraph:
30 NEW PARAGRAPH. d. (1) For municipalities of any population
31 size, and notwithstanding any minimum low and moderate income
32 family housing requirement, if the public improvement project
33 is approved on or after July 1, 2025, and is related to housing
34 and residential development in an economic development area
35 containing property that has been located entirely within the
SF 652.2002 (1) 91
(amending this SF 652 to CONFORM to HF 1037)
-1- jm/md 1/2
1 corporate limits of a city for twenty years or more, the amount
2 to be provided for low and moderate income family housing for
3 such projects shall not be required to be greater than an
4 amount equal to twenty percent of the original project cost.
5 (2) In addition, notwithstanding subsection 5, the division
6 of the revenue under section 403.19 for each project under this
7 paragraph shall be limited to tax collections for twenty fiscal
8 years beginning with the second fiscal year after the year in
9 which the municipality first certifies to the county auditor
10 the amount of any loans, advances, indebtedness, or bonds
11 which qualify for payment from the division of the revenue in
12 connection with the project.>
13 2. Title page, by striking lines 1 through 4 and inserting
14 <An Act modifying economic development provisions relating to
15 housing and residential development in urban renewal areas.>
