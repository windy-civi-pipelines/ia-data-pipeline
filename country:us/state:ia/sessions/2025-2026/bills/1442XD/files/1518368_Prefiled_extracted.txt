3 DIVISION I —— FUNCTIONS OF DEPARTMENT OF WORKFORCE
4 DEVELOPMENT, WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT BOARD, AND LOCAL WORKFORCE
5 DEVELOPMENT BOARDS. This division strikes department duties
6 relating to administering a statewide employment skills
7 assessment, selection of service providers, establishing a job
8 posting system of education-related job openings, and functions
9 no longer under the department pursuant to 2023 Iowa Acts,
10 Senate File 514.
11 The division repeals Code provisions for the Iowa
12 conservation corps, the workforce investment program, the
13 statewide mentoring program, the new employment opportunity
14 program, and the nursing workforce data clearinghouse.
15 The division generally transfers duties relating to
16 compiling an annual report for submission to the governor and
17 general assembly on various workforce training programs from
18 the department to the college student aid commission.
19 The division strikes requirements for the department to
20 collaborate with specified education-related entities when
21 performing certain duties.
22 The division strikes detailed language relating to duties
23 of the workforce development board concerning standing
24 committees, public availability of information, comprehensive
25 state workforce development plans, recommendations on the use
26 of federal funds, and various other duties. The division
27 generally provides that such duties shall be carried out as
28 required by federal law. The division similarly modifies
29 duties of local workforce development boards relating to local
30 workforce development plans.
31 DIVISION II —— UNEMPLOYMENT INSURANCE —— TAXABLE WAGES.
32 This division modifies the definition of “taxable wages”
33 for purposes of unemployment insurance taxes on employers
34 by eliminating the wages paid to an employee from another
35 state from the calculation of wages upon which an employer is
LSB 1442XD (3) 91
-9- je/js 9/10
S.F. _____ H.F. _____
1 required to contribute to the unemployment compensation fund
2 when the other state extends a like comity (reciprocity) to
3 Iowa for employment purposes. The division specifies that only
4 wages paid in Iowa can constitute taxable wages.
5 DIVISION III —— IOWA STUDENT INTERNSHIP PROGRAM. This
6 division strikes one of two components of the Iowa student
7 internship program administered by the department. The
8 component providing for financial assistance to link Iowa
9 students to small and medium-sized Iowa firms is stricken.
10 The component providing for financial assistance to place
11 Iowa students studying in the fields of science, technology,
12 engineering, and mathematics into internships that lead to
13 permanent positions with Iowa employers is retained.
14 The division appropriates $1,039,425 from the general fund
15 to the department for the Iowa student internship fund. Moneys
16 in the fund are used to increase the availability of skilled
17 workers in innovative businesses by providing Iowa student
18 internship opportunities.
19 DIVISION IV —— ADULT EDUCATION AND LITERACY PROGRAMS. Under
20 current law, the department and community colleges are required
21 to implement and set standards for certain adult education
22 and literacy programs. This division strikes references to
23 community colleges so that only the department has these
24 duties.

3 DIVISION I —— FUNCTIONS OF DEPARTMENT OF WORKFORCE
4 DEVELOPMENT, WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT BOARD, AND LOCAL WORKFORCE
5 DEVELOPMENT BOARDS. This division strikes department duties
6 relating to administering a statewide employment skills
7 assessment, selection of service providers, establishing a job
8 posting system of education-related job openings, and functions
9 no longer under the department pursuant to 2023 Iowa Acts,
10 Senate File 514.
11 The division repeals Code provisions for the Iowa
12 conservation corps, the workforce investment program, the
13 statewide mentoring program, the new employment opportunity
14 program, and the nursing workforce data clearinghouse.
15 The division generally transfers duties relating to
16 compiling an annual report for submission to the governor and
17 general assembly on various workforce training programs from
18 the department to the college student aid commission.
19 The division strikes requirements for the department to
20 collaborate with specified education-related entities when
21 performing certain duties.
22 The division strikes detailed language relating to duties
23 of the workforce development board concerning standing
24 committees, public availability of information, comprehensive
25 state workforce development plans, recommendations on the use
26 of federal funds, and various other duties. The division
27 generally provides that such duties shall be carried out as
28 required by federal law. The division similarly modifies
29 duties of local workforce development boards relating to local
30 workforce development plans.
31 DIVISION II —— UNEMPLOYMENT INSURANCE —— TAXABLE WAGES.
32 This division modifies the definition of “taxable wages”
33 for purposes of unemployment insurance taxes on employers
34 by eliminating the wages paid to an employee from another
35 state from the calculation of wages upon which an employer is
LSB 1442XD (3) 91
-9- je/js 9/10

S.F. _____ H.F. _____
1 required to contribute to the unemployment compensation fund
2 when the other state extends a like comity (reciprocity) to
3 Iowa for employment purposes. The division specifies that only
4 wages paid in Iowa can constitute taxable wages.
5 DIVISION III —— IOWA STUDENT INTERNSHIP PROGRAM. This
6 division strikes one of two components of the Iowa student
7 internship program administered by the department. The
8 component providing for financial assistance to link Iowa
9 students to small and medium-sized Iowa firms is stricken.
10 The component providing for financial assistance to place
11 Iowa students studying in the fields of science, technology,
12 engineering, and mathematics into internships that lead to
13 permanent positions with Iowa employers is retained.
14 The division appropriates $1,039,425 from the general fund
15 to the department for the Iowa student internship fund. Moneys
16 in the fund are used to increase the availability of skilled
17 workers in innovative businesses by providing Iowa student
18 internship opportunities.
19 DIVISION IV —— ADULT EDUCATION AND LITERACY PROGRAMS. Under
20 current law, the department and community colleges are required
21 to implement and set standards for certain adult education
22 and literacy programs. This division strikes references to
23 community colleges so that only the department has these
24 duties.
