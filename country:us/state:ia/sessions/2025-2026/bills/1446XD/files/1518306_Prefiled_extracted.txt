Section 1:
SENATE/HOUSE FILE _____
BY (PROPOSED DEPARTMENT OF
A BILL FOR
1 An Act relating to county commissions of veteran affairs.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF IOWA:
TLSB 1446XD (2) 91
sc/js
S.F. _____ H.F. _____
1 Section 1. Section 35A.5, subsection 14, paragraphs a and c,
2 Code 2025, are amended to read as follows:
3 a. Information related to compliance with the training
4 requirements under section 35B.6 during the previous calendar
5 fiscal year.
6 c. The number of hours of veterans’ services provided by
7 each county commission of veteran affairs executive director or
8 administrator during the previous calendar fiscal year.
9 Sec. 2. Section 35B.6, subsection 1, paragraph c,
10 subparagraphs (1) and (2), Code 2025, are amended to read as
11 follows:
12 (1) Upon the employment of an executive director or
13 administrator, the executive director or administrator shall
14 complete a course for the purpose of obtaining certification as
15 directed by the commandant of the Iowa department of veterans
16 affairs, training as federally required for the person’s
17 position, and a personal identification verification card
18 necessary to access the veterans benefits management system.
19 If an executive director or administrator fails to obtain
20 certification or a personal identification verification card
21 within one year of being employed, the executive director
22 or administrator shall be removed from office. Upon a
23 request from a county commission of veteran affairs and based
24 on extenuating circumstances, the commandant of the Iowa
25 department of veterans affairs may extend the time frame for
26 an executive director or administrator to obtain a personal
27 identification verification card.
28 (2) An executive director or administrator shall satisfy
29 the continuing education requirements established by the
30 national association of county veterans service officers
31 commandant of the Iowa department of veterans affairs and the
32 United States department of veterans affairs. Failure of an
33 executive director or administrator to maintain certification
34 shall be cause for removal from office. The expenses of
35 training the executive director or administrator shall be paid
LSB 1446XD (2) 91

S.F. _____ H.F. _____
1 from the appropriation authorized in section 35B.14.
2 EXPLANATION
3 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
4 the explanation’s substance by the members of the general assembly.
5 Under current law, the Iowa department of veterans affairs
6 is required to submit an annual report to the governor and the
7 general assembly including the county commissions of veteran
8 affairs’ training compliance and hours of veterans’ services
9 provided during the previous calendar year. This bill requires
10 the information in the report to be from the previous fiscal
11 year.
12 The bill also requires an executive director or
13 administrator of a county commission of veteran affairs to meet
14 continuing education requirements established by the commandant
15 of the Iowa department of veterans affairs and removes the
16 requirement that an executive director or administrator
17 meet the continuing education requirements of the national
18 association of county veterans service officers.
