4 <Section 1. NEW SECTION. 100B.51 Length of service award
5 programs —— authorization.
6 The governing body of a municipality, as defined in section
7 100B.21, is authorized to establish a length of service award
8 program for volunteer fire fighters as defined in section
9 85.61, emergency medical care providers as defined in section
10 147A.1 who are volunteers, and reserve peace officers as
11 defined in section 80D.1A. The program shall provide length
12 of service awards, as described in section 457(e)(11) of the
13 Internal Revenue Code, to volunteer fire fighters, volunteer
14 emergency medical care providers, and reserve peace officers
15 serving a municipality that elects to establish a program. The
16 program shall be designed to treat awards from the program as
17 a tax-deferred benefit under the Internal Revenue Code. The
18 governing body of the municipality shall, in consultation with
19 the chief or other person in command of the fire department
20 and police department serving the municipality, as applicable,
21 adopt guidelines providing for eligibility requirements for
22 participation by volunteer fire fighters, volunteer emergency
23 medical care providers, and reserve peace officers, minimum
24 vesting requirements, distribution requirements, and such other
25 guidelines as deemed necessary to operate the program.
26 Sec. 2. NEW SECTION. 100B.52 Length of service award
27 program grant fund —— appropriation.
28 1. A length of service award program grant fund is created
29 in the state treasury under the control of the department of
30 revenue. The fund shall consist of all moneys appropriated to
31 the fund.
32 2. Moneys in the length of service award program grant fund
33 are appropriated to the department of revenue for the purpose
34 of providing grants to municipalities that have established
35 a length of service award program as described in section
SF 627.2073 (3) 91
(amending this SF 627 to CONFORM to HF 1002)
-1- ns/js 1/3
1 100B.51 to provide contributions to the program on behalf
2 of participants in the program. The department of revenue
3 shall adopt rules pursuant to chapter 17A establishing a grant
4 application process. The rules must require a municipality to
5 electronically file the grant application with the department
6 of revenue. The grant process shall provide for up to a
7 dollar-for-dollar funding match from a municipality that has
8 established a length of service award program, not to exceed
9 five hundred dollars per person in the program receiving the
10 grant. If the amount in the fund is insufficient to pay all the
11 eligible grants in a fiscal year, the director of revenue shall
12 prorate the moneys awarded to each municipality. The grant
13 process shall allow a municipality to use moneys received to
14 fund the program from gifts, devises, bequests, or any other
15 source for purposes of providing the funding match required by
16 this subsection.
17 3. Notwithstanding section 12C.7, subsection 2, interest or
18 earnings on moneys deposited in the fund shall be credited to
19 the fund. Notwithstanding section 8.33, moneys credited to the
20 fund shall not revert at the close of a fiscal year.
21 Sec. 3. APPROPRIATION.
22 1. There is appropriated from the sports wagering receipts
23 fund created in section 8.57I to the department of revenue for
24 the fiscal year beginning July 1, 2025, and ending June 30,
25 2026, the following amount, or so much thereof as is necessary,
26 to be used for the purposes designated:
27 For deposit in the length of service award program grant fund
28 created in section 100B.52, as enacted by this Act:
29 .................................................. $ 1,500,000
30 2. Moneys received from the sports wagering receipts fund
31 pursuant to this section are not subject to the reporting
32 requirements provided in section 8.57I, subsections 4 and 5.>
