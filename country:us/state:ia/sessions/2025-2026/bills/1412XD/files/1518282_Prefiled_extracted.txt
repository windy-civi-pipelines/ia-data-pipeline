S.F. _____ H.F. _____
1 Section 1. Section 43.37, Code 2025, is amended to read as
2 follows:
3 43.37 Number of votes permitted per office —— prohibited
4 methods.
5 1. The elector shall be permitted to vote for no more
6 candidates for any office than there are persons to be elected
7 to the office. If an elector votes for more persons for any
8 office than the number permitted, the elector’s ballot shall
9 not be counted for that office.
10 2. a. An election in this state shall not be conducted
11 using ranked choice voting or instant runoff voting, including
12 an election for any statewide, local government, or other
13 office.
14 b. For purposes of this subsection:
15 (1) “Local government” means an incorporated city or town,
16 an unincorporated city or town, a county, a school, or any
17 other political subdivision of the state.
18 (2) “Ranked choice voting” or “instant runoff voting” means
19 a method of casting and tabulating votes in which a voter
20 ranks candidates in order of preference, tabulation of ballots
21 proceeds in rounds such that in each round either a candidate
22 is elected or the candidate receiving the fewest votes is
23 defeated, votes are transferred from elected or defeated
24 candidates to a voter’s next-ranked candidate in order of
25 preference, and tabulation ends when a candidate receives the
26 majority of votes cast or the number of candidates elected
27 equals the number of offices to be filled, as applicable.
28 Sec. 2. Section 49.93, Code 2025, is amended to read as
29 follows:
30 49.93 Number of votes for each office —— prohibited methods.
31 1. For an office to which one person is to be elected, a
32 voter shall not vote for more than one candidate. If two or
33 more persons are to be elected to an office, the voter shall
34 vote for no more than the number of persons to be elected. If a
35 person votes for more than the permitted number of candidates,

22 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
23 the explanation’s substance by the members of the general assembly.
24 This bill prohibits ranked choice and instant runoff voting,
25 defined in the bill, from being used to cast or tabulate
26 ballots in any election in this state, including in elections
27 for local government offices, defined in the bill.
