19 EXPLANATION
20 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
21 the explanation’s substance by the members of the general assembly.
22 This bill relates to cigarette and tobacco product
23 regulations including vapor products by requiring the
24 electronic filing of returns and other related matters.
25 The bill specifies that cities and counties may approve
26 tobacco-related retail permits but the department shall issue
27 the permit to the applicant on behalf of the city or county.
28 The bill requires the city or county to use the electronic
29 portal of the department to process retail permit applications.
30 If a city or county is unable to use the electronic portal
31 of the department, the city or county may request permission
32 from the director of revenue to process retail applications by
33 another method.
34 The bill provides that all tobacco-related permit fees shall
35 be collected by the department, and the department shall credit
LSB 1387XD (6) 91
-15- jm/jh 15/16
S.F. _____ H.F. _____
1 the fees to the city or county where the permit is in effect.
2 The bill specifies that a person who fails to timely file or
3 submit a required return, report, or other documentation upon
4 which no tax is shown due is subject to a penalty in the amount
5 of $50 for each occurrence.
6 The bill requires cigarette and tobacco tax returns,
7 reports, invoices, bonds, and payments to be filed with or

19 EXPLANATION
20 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
21 the explanation’s substance by the members of the general assembly.
22 This bill relates to cigarette and tobacco product
23 regulations including vapor products by requiring the
24 electronic filing of returns and other related matters.
25 The bill specifies that cities and counties may approve
26 tobacco-related retail permits but the department shall issue
27 the permit to the applicant on behalf of the city or county.
28 The bill requires the city or county to use the electronic
29 portal of the department to process retail permit applications.
30 If a city or county is unable to use the electronic portal
31 of the department, the city or county may request permission
32 from the director of revenue to process retail applications by
33 another method.
34 The bill provides that all tobacco-related permit fees shall
35 be collected by the department, and the department shall credit
LSB 1387XD (6) 91
-15- jm/jh 15/16

S.F. _____ H.F. _____
1 the fees to the city or county where the permit is in effect.
2 The bill specifies that a person who fails to timely file or
3 submit a required return, report, or other documentation upon
4 which no tax is shown due is subject to a penalty in the amount
5 of $50 for each occurrence.
6 The bill requires cigarette and tobacco tax returns,
7 reports, invoices, bonds, and payments to be filed with or
8 submitted to the department electronically beginning on or
9 after July 1, 2025.
10 The bill provides any report, return, remittance, or other
11 documentation required to be submitted electronically that is
12 submitted in a manner other than in an electronic format shall
13 be considered not a valid submission. However, the bill does
14 allow a person required to make a submission electronically to
15 request permission from the director of revenue to make the
16 submission in another manner.
17 The bill directs the Code editor to place new Code section
18 453A.57 (submitting documents —— alternative method) in
19 subchapter IV.
