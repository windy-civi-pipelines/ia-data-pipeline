4 amended to read as follows:
5 1. Except as otherwise provided, for purposes of this
6 chapter and chapter 6B:
7 a. “Aboveground merchant line” means “merchant line” as
8 defined in section 478.6A, subsection 1, excluding those
9 merchant lines that are underground.
10 b. “Agricultural land” means real property owned by a person
11 in tracts of ten acres or more and not laid off into lots of
12 less than ten acres or divided by streets and alleys into
13 parcels of less than ten acres, and that has been used for
14 the production of agricultural commodities during three out
15 of the past five years. Such use of property includes, but
16 is not limited to, the raising, harvesting, handling, drying,
17 or storage of crops used for feed, food, seed, or fiber; the
18 care or feeding of livestock; the handling or transportation
19 of crops or livestock; the storage, treatment, or disposal
20 of livestock manure; and the application of fertilizers,
21 soil conditioners, pesticides, and herbicides on crops.
22 Agricultural land includes land on which is located farm
23 residences or outbuildings used for agricultural purposes and
24 land on which is located facilities, structures, or equipment
25 for agricultural purposes. Agricultural land includes
26 land taken out of agricultural production for purposes of
27 environmental protection or preservation.
28 c. “Commodity” means a product that is used by an individual
29 consumer or is used to produce a product used by an individual
30 consumer.
31 d. “Common carrier” means a commercial enterprise that holds
32 itself out as ready to engage in the transportation of goods
33 or passengers for hire, as a public employment, and not as a
34 casual occupation, and that undertakes to carry for all persons
35 indifferently, within the limits of the enterprise’s capacity
HF 639.1220 (3) 91
-1- sb/js 1/3
1 and the sphere of business required of it. For a carrier
2 engaged in the transportation of a hazardous liquid to qualify
3 as a common carrier, the carrier must establish by clear and
4 convincing evidence that it will transport a commodity for
5 one or more shippers not affiliated with the carrier who
6 will either retain ownership of the commodity or sell the
7 commodity to a party other than the carrier. A common carrier
8 determination by the federal energy regulatory commission shall
9 be controlling for purposes of this paragraph.
10 c. e. “Private development purposes” means the
11 construction of, or improvement related to, recreational
12 trails, recreational development paid for primarily with
13 private funds, aboveground merchant lines, housing and
14 residential development, or commercial or industrial enterprise
15 development.
16 d. f. “Public use” or “public purpose” or “public
17 improvement” does not include the authority to condemn
18 agricultural land for private development purposes unless the
19 owner of the agricultural land consents to the condemnation.

27 purpose, or public improvement.>
