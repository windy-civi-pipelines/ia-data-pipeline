3 Sec. ___. NEW SECTION. 261K.2 Diversity, equity, and
4 inclusion office prohibited.
5 1. A private institution of higher education shall not,
6 except as otherwise provided by federal or state law or
7 accreditation standards, establish, sustain, support, or staff
8 a diversity, equity, and inclusion office.
9 2. Subsection 1 shall not be construed to cover or affect a
10 private institution of higher education’s funding of any of the
11 following:
12 a. Academic course instruction.
13 b. Research or creative works by the private institution
14 of higher education’s students, faculty, or other research
15 personnel, and the dissemination of such research or creative
16 works.
17 c. Activities of registered student organizations.
18 d. Arrangements for guest speakers and performers with
19 short-term engagements.
20 e. Mental or physical health services provided by licensed
21 professionals.
22 3. Subsection 1 shall not be construed as prohibiting bona
23 fide qualifications based on sex that are reasonably necessary
24 to the normal operation of private higher education.
25 Sec. ___. NEW SECTION. 261K.3 Enforcement.
26 1. Any person may report a private institution of higher
27 education’s potential violation of section 261K.2 to the
28 attorney general. The attorney general shall provide notice
29 of such report to the department and the institution within
30 fifteen days. No later than thirty days after receiving such
31 notice, the institution shall do one of the following:
32 a. Correct the violation and provide documentation of the
33 correction to the attorney general and the department.
34 b. Provide documentation to the attorney general and the
35 department demonstrating that the action of the institution

1 upon which the report was based was not a violation of section
2 261K.2.
3 2. If, upon the conclusion of the thirty-day period
4 provided in subsection 1, the institution has not complied
5 with subsection 1, paragraph “a”, or provided documentation
6 under subsection 1, paragraph “b”, that demonstrates to
7 the satisfaction of the attorney general that a violation
8 of section 261K.2 did not occur, the institution shall be
9 ineligible to participate in the tuition grant program under
10 chapter 256, subchapter VII, part 4, subpart B, as of the
11 next academic year beginning thereafter. If either action is
12 carried out by the institution by October 1 of an academic
13 year, eligibility shall be restored as of the next academic
14 year beginning after such action is carried out. The attorney
15 general shall notify the college student aid commission to this
16 effect.>
