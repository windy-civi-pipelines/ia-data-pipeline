Section 28M.2, subsection 1, Code 2025, is 1 amended to read as follows: 2 1. A county with a population in excess of one hundred 3 seventy-five thousand and participating cities may create, 4 by chapter 28E agreement, a regional transit district in the 5 county pursuant to this chapter . Two or more contiguous 6 counties and participating cities may create, by chapter 7 28E agreement, a regional transit district pursuant to this 8 chapter if one of the counties has a population in excess of 9 one hundred seventy-five thousand . A district shall consist 10 of the unincorporated area of any participating county and the 11 incorporated area of any city in the county that does not have 12 an urban transit system. However, a city without an urban 13 transit system may decline, by resolution forwarded to the 14 board of supervisors, to participate in a regional transit 15 district. 16 EXPLANATION 17 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with 18 the explanationâs substance by the members of the general assembly. 19 A regional transit district is a public transit district 20 created pursuant to Code chapter 28E by one or more counties 21 and participating cities to provide support for transportation 22 of passengers by one or more public transit systems that may be 23 designated as a public transit system under Code chapter 324A. 24 Under current law, a regional transit district can be created 25 by one or more counties, if at least one of the counties has 26 a population in excess of 175,000, and participating cities 27 through a Code chapter 28E agreement. This bill eliminates the 28 county population threshold to be able to create a regional 29 transit district with participating cities. 30 -1- LSB 2644YC (2) 91 ll/js 1/ 1
