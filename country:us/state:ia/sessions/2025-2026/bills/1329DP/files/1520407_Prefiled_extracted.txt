1 Section 1. NEW SECTION. 8A.311C State purchasing contracts
2 —— prohibited terms.
3 The provisions set forth in this section are void as a matter
4 of law as contrary to public policy if included in a contract
5 entered into by the department, the department of management or
6 a supported entity pursuant to chapter 8, subchapter XI, or any
7 other state agency. A state agency’s contract that includes
8 any of the following provisions shall be interpreted as if the
9 contract did not include the void provision:
10 1. A provision that requires the state or its agencies to
11 defend, indemnify, hold harmless another person, or otherwise
12 assume the debt or liability of another person in violation of
13 Article VII, section 1, of the Constitution of the State of
14 Iowa.
15 2. A provision that seeks to impose vendor terms that
16 are unknown at the time of signing the contract or can be
17 unilaterally changed by the vendor.
18 3. A provision that violates chapter 13 by not allowing
19 a state agency to participate in its own defense through
20 representation by the attorney general.
21 4. A provision that grants to any person other than the
22 attorney general the authority to convey to a court or litigant
23 the state’s consent to any settlement of a suit involving the
24 contract when such settlement could impose liability on the
25 state.
26 5. A provision that specifies that the contract is governed
27 by the laws of a foreign state or nation.
28 6. A provision that claims blanket confidentiality of the
29 contract’s terms.
30 7. A provision that claims that payment terms, including but
31 not limited to cost proposals or other pricing information, of
32 the contract are confidential.
33 8. A provision that authorizes or requires a venue for
34 litigation other than the district court of Polk county, Iowa,
35 or the United States district court for the southern district

1 of Iowa sitting in Des Moines, Iowa.
2 9. A provision that requires a state agency to pay attorney
3 fees, court costs, or other litigation expenses in the event of
4 a contractual dispute.
5 10. A provision that imposes on the state or its agencies
6 binding arbitration or another binding extrajudicial dispute
7 resolution process in which the final resolution is not
8 determined by the state.
9 11. A provision that waives a state agency’s right to a jury
10 trial.
11 12. A provision that obligates a state agency to pay a late
12 payment charge not consistent with section 8A.514, interest
13 greater than allowed under section 8A.514 or other applicable
14 law, or any cancellation charge, as such charges constitute
15 pledges of the state’s credit.
16 13. A provision that obligates a state agency to pay a tax.
17 14. A provision that imposes a prior notice obligation on
18 a state agency as a condition for the automatic renewal of a
19 software license. A state agency may provide notice of its
20 intent to terminate a software license at any time before the
21 renewal date established in the contract.
22 15. A provision that obligates a state agency to accept risk
23 of loss before the receipt of items or goods.
24 16. A provision that obligates a state agency to have
25 commercial insurance.
26 17. A provision that obligates a state agency to grant a
27 contractor full or partial ownership of intellectual property
28 developed pursuant to a state agency contract when the
29 intellectual property is developed in whole or in part using
30 federal funding.
31 18. A provision that limits the time in which the state or
32 its agencies may bring a legal claim under the contract to a
33 period shorter than that provided by Iowa law.
34 19. A boilerplate provision included in a vendor’s
35 transactional documents, including but not limited to ordering

1 documents and quotations, that seeks to alter the terms of a
2 state agency’s contract or to impose new terms in the contract.
3 Sec. 2. NEW SECTION. 8A.311D State purchasing contracts ——
4 required terms.
5 All of the following provisions shall be deemed to be
6 included in a state agency vendor contract:
7 1. Governing law. The contract shall be governed by
8 the laws of the state of Iowa, without giving effect to any
9 conflict of laws principles of Iowa law that may require the
10 application of another jurisdiction’s law.
11 2. Venue. Any litigation commenced in connection with the
12 contract shall be brought and maintained in the district court
13 of Polk county, Iowa, or the United States district court for
14 the southern district of Iowa sitting in Des Moines, Iowa, as
15 appropriate.
16 Sec. 3. APPLICABILITY. This Act applies to contracts
17 entered into or renewed on or after the effective date of this
18 Act.
19 EXPLANATION
20 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
21 the explanation’s substance by the members of the general assembly.
22 This bill prohibits the inclusion of certain provisions in
23 contracts entered into by the department of administrative
24 services, the department of management or supported entities
25 pursuant to Code chapter 8, subchapter XI (information
26 technology), and any other state agency, and declares those
27 provisions void if present in such contracts. The bill also
28 provides that every state agency vendor contract shall be
29 deemed to include provisions requiring the contract to be
30 governed by Iowa law and for litigation related to the contract
31 to be brought and maintained in the district court of Polk
32 county, Iowa, or the United States district court for the
33 southern district of Iowa sitting in Des Moines, Iowa.
