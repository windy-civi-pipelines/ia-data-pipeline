House File 819 - Introduced HOUSE FILE 819 BY YOUNG , ZABNER , MATSON , and DUNWELL A BILL FOR An Act relating to an absentee ballot marking pilot program for 1 voters with disabilities. 2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF IOWA: 3 TLSB 2670YH (2) 91 ss/ns H.F. 819 Section 1. NEW SECTION . 53.5 Absentee ballot marking pilot 1 program. 2 1. The state commissioner may adopt rules pursuant 3 to chapter 17A to implement a pilot program to establish 4 procedures to enable a voter with a disability that prevents 5 the voter from marking a ballot by hand to independently and 6 privately mark a ballot or use an electronic voting device that 7 produces a paper record using nonvisual access, low-vision 8 access, or other assistive technology in order for the voter to 9 complete an absentee ballot by mail pursuant to this chapter. 10 The procedures must include a method by which a voter with a 11 disability that prevents the voter from marking a ballot by 12 hand to request an absentee ballot by mail. 13 2. If the pilot program under this section is not 14 implemented statewide, the pilot program must include a 15 sufficient number of counties, as determined by the state 16 commissioner, to conclude whether the program is feasible 17 statewide. 18 3. The state commissioner shall file a report with the 19 general assembly by December 31 of each year regarding the 20 pilot program established under this section. 21 4. This section is repealed July 1, 2028. 22 EXPLANATION 23 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with 24 the explanationâs substance by the members of the general assembly. 25 This bill allows the state commissioner of elections to 26 adopt rules to establish a pilot program to allow voters with 27 disabilities that prevent such voters from marking ballots by 28 hand to independently and privately mark absentee ballots, 29 including by the use of assistive technology. The bill 30 requires that the program include a method by which such voters 31 can request an absentee ballot by mail. 32 If the pilot program under the bill is not established 33 statewide, the bill requires that the program be established 34 in a sufficient number of counties to allow the state 35 -1- LSB 2670YH (2) 91 ss/ns 1/ 2 H.F. 819 commissioner of elections to determine whether the program is 1 feasible statewide. The bill requires the state commissioner 2 of elections to file a report with the general assembly 3 by December 31 of each year regarding the pilot program 4 established under the bill. 5 The pilot program is repealed July 1, 2028. 6 -2- LSB 2670YH (2) 91 ss/ns 2/ 2
